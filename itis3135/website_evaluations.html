Website Evaluations

Good Website:

Apple

Apple is a technology company known for its sleek and user-friendly design across its products and website.

  • Contrast: Apple’s website uses high-quality visuals, ample white space, and clear typography, creating a modern and elegant design that highlights products effectively.
  • Repitition: The consistent use of fonts, colors, and layouts ensures a seamless and recognizable brand experience across all pages.
  • Alignment: A structured grid system keeps all elements perfectly organized, making navigation smooth and visually appealing.
  • Proximity: Product details, descriptions, and images are well-grouped, making it easy for users to browse.

Bad Website:

ebay

eBay is an online marketplace where users can buy and sell products through auctions or direct purchases.

  • Contrast: eBay’s site is visually cluttered with small text, overwhelming product listings, and excessive advertisements, making it difficult to focus.
  • Repitition: While branding and colors remain consistent, there is too much variation in text size, button styles, and layout.
  • Alignment: The homepage and product pages feel cluttered with too many sections competing for attention, often making the alignment appear messy and inconsistent.
  • Proximity: Too much information is crammed into a single page, making it difficult to scan and navigate effectively.
